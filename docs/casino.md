# Casino 

The utility I added here is a fun little gambling game called **“Casino”** (I know — crazy).  
When you run the command-line `casino`, it will give you **four options**:

---

## 1.  Play Craps

Craps is a dice game where you roll two six-sided dice. Here's how it works:

- On your **first roll**:
  - Roll a **7 or 11** → **you win**
  - Roll a **2, 3, or 12** → **you lose** (that’s craps)
  - Any other number (4, 5, 6, 8, 9, or 10) becomes your **"point"**

- After that, keep rolling until:
  - You roll your **point** again → **you win**
  - You roll a **7** → **you lose**

It’s a fast-paced and very simple game :)

---

## 2.  Play Blackjack

Blackjack is a game where you play **against the dealer (the house)**, trying to get as close to **21** as possible without going over.

- Both you and the dealer start with **two cards** — but you only see **one** of the dealer's cards.

### Card values:
- Number cards: face value (2–10)  
- Face cards (J, Q, K): 10  
- Aces: **1 or 11**, whichever helps you more

### Your move options:
- **Hit (h)**: take another card  
- **Stand (s)**: end your turn  
- **Split (p)**: if your two cards are the same, split into two hands and play both

After you stand (or bust), the dealer reveals their second card and plays:
- The dealer must **hit until reaching at least 17**

Whoever is closer to 21 without going over **wins**.  

My personal favorite so you should try it out!

---

## 3.  Play Slots

This is a **pure chance** game:

- Hit return and the slot machine will **randomly roll three symbols**.

### Outcomes:
- Match 2 -> **Small win!**  
- Match 3 -> **Jackpot!**  
- Match nothing -> **Better luck next time!**

No strategy here — just luck. 

---

## 4.  Cash Out / Quit

If you're done playing, you can cash out and leave the casino.  
But really, **why would you ever want to quit this utility?**
